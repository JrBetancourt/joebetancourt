    Design

    Our team decided to go with a square ice chest to optimize storage space for beverages and for the cooling apparatus to function at peak performance and efficiency. Using Solidworks our team created multiple renderings to conceptualize our design.

    Design Overview

    The design is comprised of two components: the gear system designed by Average Joe's Inc., and a traditional cooler to provide optimal insulation. Our gears were designed using Solidworks and a 3D printer which allowed us to create and test several designs. We determined a 1:3 gear ratio with the driving gear having 72 teeth and driven gear having 24 teeth was optimal for this design.

    Engineering Analysis

    Using the heat capacity formula equation 1 and an initial temperature of 74°F with a final temperature of 32°F we equated 30.19Kj to reach optimal temperature in a 2 minute period. This is achieved through the convection of the beverage liquid, conduction of the aluminum can and convection from the ice water.

    The concept behind the Crank-N-Chill is to apply high revolutions to an aluminum can, causing centripetal force to liquid inside the beverage can causing forced convection. This will increase the convective heat transfer coefficient (h), which increases the rate at which heat is transferred.     Proof-Of-Concept

    We did three 2 minute trials with a 12oz 7up in ice water. Unfortunately, our results fell short from our desired temperature of 32°F. Spinning the beverages at a high rate did in fact axcelerate heat transfer but many factors may have caused us not obtaining our desired temperature in the given time frame. A few examples include arm fatigue, internal beverage liquid not at 72°F, ice water not desired temp or faulty cooler. Ways we could have improved is adding a motor to the system, nitrogen or salt water, or a better insulated cooler.

    Conclusion

    Ultimately, the Crank-N-Chill has been a fun and engaging project. Our team has met all expectations in meeting the main objective in building the Crank-N-Chill.
